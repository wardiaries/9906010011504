Mission, Vision, and Goals

Mission:

To provide a competitive and inclusive platform for PUBG Mobile players, promoting talent, fair play, and celebrating the spirit of esports.

Vision:

To be a leading organizer of esports tournaments, known for honing sportsmanship, transparency, and professionalism.

Goals:

Promote fair competition and uphold the integrity of all matches.Create opportunities for amateur and professional players to showcase their skills. Provide a transparent and organized tournament environment. Support the growth of esports through responsible event management. Ensure fair compensation and recognition for the efforts of staff and participants.

1. Eligibility and Payments

1.1
Participants must register before the deadline set by the tournament organizers.

1.2
Each player must use their main account during the tournament. Use of alternative accounts without prior approval is prohibited.

1.3
Players must be part of an officially registered team. Solo participants will not be allowed unless otherwise stated in special formats.

If a low-level account is present at any point during a match‚Äîeven if they leave or exit early‚Äîthat match‚Äôs score will be considered void and not counted for the team. Confirmed payments are non-refundable but transferable.

8. ROOM FORMATS

Room Waiting Time

Room waiting time will only last for a minimum of five (5) minutes and a maximum of seven (7) minutes. Game or player settings may be adjusted by the Tournament Organizer at any time before a match begins, as deemed necessary.

Tiebreaker Rules
WWCD (Winner Winner Chicken Dinner)

The team with the most WWCDs wins the tie.

Placement Points

If still tied, the team with the higher placement points ranks higher.

Total Kills

If still tied, the team with the most total kills ranks higher.

Other Criteria

If still tied, the final tiebreaker will be based on overall performance in the latest/last match, head-to-head results, or longest average survival time.

MVP

Damage points will be the tiebreaker.

9. JUSTIFICATION OF FEES

9.1 Assistant Administrators / Moderators / Coordinators Fee

Role Overview: Assistant Administrators, Moderators, or Coordinators support the Tournament Head by managing critical administrative and operational tasks. Their responsibilities include participant registration, data management, and ensuring smooth communication between teams, officials, and international partners. They play a vital role in maintaining organization and upholding tournament policies.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Facilitate player/team registration and verify eligibility
  • Maintain accurate participant and match records
  • Coordinate with international teams and officials for cross-border events
  • Enforce tournament rules and address participant inquiries
  • Assist in scheduling and operational planning
9.2 Match Facilitators / Moderators Fee

Role Overview: Match Facilitators or Moderators oversee the execution of individual matches to ensure fair play and timely progression. They are responsible for creating and managing game rooms/lobbies, monitoring match conduct, and resolving any in-game issues that arise.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Create and manage match rooms/lobbies according to the schedule
  • Communicate with teams to confirm readiness and resolve conflicts
  • Monitor matches for adherence to rules and fair play
  • Report match results and escalate disputes to higher officials when necessary
  • Assist in coordinating technical aspects, such as server or platform issues
9.3 Leaderboard Handlers Fee

Role Overview: Leaderboard Handlers manage the compilation, updating, and dissemination of tournament standings. They ensure that results from matches are accurately reflected in the rankings, providing transparent and timely updates to players, staff, and audiences.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Collect and verify match results from facilitators and referees
  • Update leaderboard standings promptly and accurately
  • Manage tie-breakers, points calculation, and ranking criteria
  • Prepare visual or digital leaderboards for publication on tournament platforms
  • Coordinate with the communications team to ensure public access to updated standings
9.4 Graphic Assets Fee

Payment for graphic assets, including banners (elimination round team lists), finals banners, team roster posters, and production assets. Covers only tournament-related items. A portion may be covered externally by sponsors, the rest from tournament funds. Includes downpayment for the introductory banner for the next tournament.

9.5 Caster Fee

Payment for the caster during the finals (amount may vary depending on the Caster Fee).

9.6 Logistics Expenses Fees

Description: Costs associated with technical and operational support necessary to ensure smooth running of tournament matches.

Key Expense Items:

  • Room Cards (Game Room Slots): Fees paid to secure or rent game room slots or keys for matches.
  • Emergency Load (Buffer): Reserved funds for unexpected expenses or delays during the tournament.
9.7 Misc. Fees
  • Emergency Load: For unforeseen internet disconnection of any room host during the event.
  • Transaction Fee: For prize disbursement via bank transfer, remittance, PayPal, etc.
9.8 Budget Flexibility

In the event that the target number of registered teams is not met, the tournament budget will be adjusted accordingly. Financial allocations‚Äîincluding prize pool, staff compensation, and production expenses‚Äîwill be modified based on actual collected funds. This ensures financial sustainability while maintaining fairness and transparency.

Note: The head organizer does not receive compensation from tournament funds; only admins under the head organizer are eligible.

The tournament operates solely on registration fees. All expenses, including production, graphics, and staff stipends, are allocated to ensure a high-quality experience with transparent fund distribution. Any surplus or shortage is reflected accordingly.

We aim to minimize expenses without compromising fair compensation for admins and staff. Priority is to maintain a high-quality event while ensuring sustainability. We follow a 60% prize pool / 40% expenses threshold to allocate a substantial portion to the prize pool while covering production costs and staff compensation.

During preliminary registration, the budget projection is conservative to cover all potential expenses. Once running, the budget becomes actual-based, adjusting according to final collected funds and actual expenses.
